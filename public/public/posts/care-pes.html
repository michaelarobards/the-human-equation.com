The CARE-PES Framework

Guiding ethical AI use in mental health and beyond

The first time I sat across from an engineer eager to build a “therapist bot,” I felt both excited and uneasy. Excited because technology can help more people access support; uneasy because therapy is about relationship, humility and culture — things machines don’t naturally grasp. I knew we needed a map to guide these conversations. That map became CARE‑PES, an ethical framework that keeps humans at the center of AI in mental health. Each letter is an invitation to slow down and reflect before launching a tool into someone’s most intimate struggles.

Cultural sensitivity: No algorithm is neutral. People express pain, joy, anger and love differently across cultures. When we develop or deploy an AI system, we ask: does it honor Indigenous parenting models as much as Western ones? Does it understand that family roles and intimacy look different in Asian, Latinx or neurodiverse households? If the answer is “I’m not sure,” the tool isn’t ready.

Accessibility: Technology can widen access or create new barriers. We test our interfaces with elders who have never used smartphones, with people who read at a fourth‑grade level, with those who need screen readers and those living in rural areas with low bandwidth. If a tool can’t serve them, it doesn’t serve our mission.

Reliability: AI outputs aren’t gospel. They reflect patterns in historical data — patterns that may be biased or outdated. Every recommendation must be reviewed by a clinician who knows the client’s history. We treat AI like a second opinion: useful, but never a replacement for professional judgment.

Empathy: Even a chatbot can leave you feeling more seen or more dismissed. We design responses that say, “I hear you,” not “I’ve calculated your risk score.” The tone, pacing and language of AI matters. A cold script can retraumatize; a warm, attuned response can offer a bridge back to connection.

Privacy: Every digital tool is a window into someone’s life. We insist on consent and transparency: clients must know what data is collected, how long it’s stored and who has access. We default to opt‑in, and we refuse to monetize personal stories.

Equity: Machine learning reproduces social bias unless you fight against it. We test our models across gender, race, ability, sexuality and socioeconomic status. If outcomes differ, we pause development to correct it. Liberation isn’t a feature request — it’s the baseline.

Synergy: At its best, technology isn’t a competitor to human connection; it’s a companion. An ADHD client might use a journaling bot to track emotions between sessions, then bring that data into therapy. A couple might use an AI‑assisted values exercise to reveal hidden priorities, then discuss it with their counselor. Synergy means AI and therapists make each other better.

"Ethical AI isn’t about shiny features; it’s about humility — remembering that software should serve people, culture and healing."

At The Human Equation we use CARE‑PES as a checklist every time we consider technology. If a tool can’t meet these standards, we don’t recommend it. By combining evidence‑based practice with ethical AI, we widen access to support without sacrificing the heart of our work: relationship.
