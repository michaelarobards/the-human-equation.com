Community Guidelines & Terms of Use

Our agreements for showing up with care, respect, and integrity.

Why We Have Guidelines

The Human Equation is not just a platform—it’s a living community. In order to protect the emotional safety of every person who shows up, we have agreements about how we speak to each other, how we share information, and how we hold space. Please read these guidelines carefully before participating in any of our groups, workshops, or online spaces.

Respect & Confidentiality

Our groups are confidential and judgment-free spaces. Everything shared by members stays within the group—no recording, screenshotting, or retelling of another person’s story. Speak to others with respect, curiosity, and compassion. We do not tolerate shaming, name-calling, or discriminatory language of any kind.

This Is Not Therapy

The Human Equation provides psychoeducational groups and peer support. While Michael A. RoBards, LCSW is a licensed clinician, these community programs are not a substitute for individual psychotherapy or emergency mental‑health care. If you are in crisis, please contact local emergency services, a crisis hotline, or your personal therapist. Our groups do not constitute a therapist–client relationship.

Participation & Attendance

Groups work best when people show up consistently. If you commit to a membership tier that includes weekly sessions, we ask that you attend as often as possible and communicate proactively if you will miss a session. Late arrivals and “ghosting” the group negatively impact the container. If you anticipate a long absence, please discuss options with your facilitator.

Listening & Sharing

When someone else is speaking, listen without planning your response. We practice non‑interruption and reflective listening. Share from your own experience using “I” statements rather than giving unsolicited advice. If you are uncertain about what’s appropriate to share, ask the facilitator.

Leadership & Boundaries

Some groups rotate leadership among members. When it’s your turn to facilitate, we provide structure and support. Facilitators maintain time boundaries, guide the conversation, and ensure that no single person dominates the space. If conflict arises, bring it to the facilitator privately so it can be handled with care.

Intellectual Property & Materials

The Insight Game, Identity Pie, CARE‑PES framework, and other materials shared in our programs are intellectual property owned by The Human Equation, The Leadership Dimension, and the Estate of Martine J. RoBards, Ph.D. Please do not reproduce or distribute these materials without written permission. They are offered for your personal growth and not for commercial use.

Acknowledgment & Agreement

By participating in The Human Equation’s groups, workshops, or online community, you agree to abide by these guidelines. If a facilitator feels that a participant is not aligned with our values, they may be asked to leave the group or modify their behavior. We strive to protect the safety and dignity of every member.

If you have questions or concerns about these guidelines, please contact us at michael@the-human-equation.com.
